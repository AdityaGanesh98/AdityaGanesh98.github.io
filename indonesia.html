The Anak Krakatau volcano is situated at the Ring of Fire—the area in the Pacific Ocean that is home to many earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. The 40,000 km horseshoe shaped area has 452 volcanoes and is associated with high tectonic activity. Volcanoes along the ring are formed by the process called subduction—when one plate gets shoved under another into the mantle. Mantle is the rocky solid body that lies between Earth’s crust and the molten iron core. Tsunami-causing large earthquakes can also occur in the subduction zones and Indonesia is formed in part by the volcanoes in this very Ring of Fire.
